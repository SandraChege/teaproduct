The poularity of tea continues to grow rapidly in the world. The history of tea dates back to ancient China. According to legend, Emperor Shen Nung discovered tea when leaves from a tree fell into his pot of boiling water. He was immediately entraced in the pleasant scent of the resulting brew, and drank some. Legend says the Emperor described a warm feeling as he drank the intriguing brew, as if the liquid was investigating every part of his body.

afternoon tea

"Afternoon tea" in Britain is a real tradition.It began among wealthy women in the 1800s, but soon spread to hotels, homes, and tea gardens across Great Britain.The tea is typically served with small sandwiches, biscuits or sweets.

Cha Dao, translated as “the way of tea”, is an ancient practice with a long-standing lineage of tea sages, masters and teachers. Cha-dao is an art which brings aesthetic pleasure, and it is also a method of self-cultivation to the Chinese people. It has a bitter taste after taking a sip but a sweet savourly taste is left lingering after swallowing.

thai iced tea

Thai iced tea or cha-yen is perhaps the most famous brew in thailand-culture It's made from strongly brewed black Ceylon tea, and blended with condensed milk and sugar before being served over ice. Various flavours or spices are added. These can include orange blossom, cinnamon, star anise, liquorice, and ground tamarind.
